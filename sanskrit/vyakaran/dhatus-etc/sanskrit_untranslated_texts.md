# Untranslated & Rarely Translated Sanskrit Texts
### A Companion Volume to the Comprehensive Sanskrit Literature Guide

> **Legend for Translation Status**
> - 🔴 **Untranslated** — No English translation known to exist
> - 🟠 **Fragmentary** — Only partial sections or excerpts translated
> - 🟡 **Rare/Obscure** — Translated once, out of print, or in non-English languages only
> - 🟢 **Recently Translated** — Modern translation exists but poorly known

> **Primary Manuscript Resources**
> - [Muktabodha Digital Library](https://muktabodha.org) — Tantric & Āgamic manuscripts
> - [National Mission for Manuscripts (NMM)](https://namami.nic.in) — Indian manuscript repositories
> - [Wellcome Collection Sanskrit MSS](https://wellcomecollection.org) — Medical texts
> - [Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute (BORI)](https://www.bori.ac.in) — Critical editions
> - [French Institute of Pondicherry (IFP)](https://www.ifpindia.org) — Śaiva Āgamas
> - [GRETIL](https://gretil.sub.uni-goettingen.de/gretil.html) — Electronic texts
> - [SARIT](https://sarit.indology.info) — Searchable critical editions

## Key Research Gaps

The following areas have the **highest density of untranslated material** and offer the most opportunity for future scholarship:

1. **Pañcarātra Āgamas** — 200+ texts, foundational for Vaiṣṇava temple ritual across South and Southeast Asia
2. **Śaiva Āgamas** — 28 primary + 207 sub-Āgamas; most only available in Tamil/French critical editions
3. **Nibandha literature** — Medieval legal digests like Hemādri's *Caturvargacintāmaṇi* (4 vols.) are the size of encyclopaedias
4. **Skandapurāṇa** — One of the largest Sanskrit texts ever written; the Groningen Project has produced critical editions but translations lag far behind
5. **Sthalapurāṇas / Māhātmyas** — Estimated 3,000+ texts; none systematically catalogued in translation
6. **Rasaśāstra (Alchemy)** — A major Indian intellectual tradition barely studied outside India
7. **Paddhati texts** — Ritual handbooks used daily in temples; thousands of MSS, almost zero translations
8. **Regional praśastis** — Hundreds of Sanskrit literary inscriptions that are primary historical sources
