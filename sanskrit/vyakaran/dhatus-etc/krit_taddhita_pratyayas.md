# Kṛt and Taddhita Pratyayas — Daily Usage Reference

*Organized by function rather than Pāṇinian sūtra order, for practical use.*
*Examples given in nominative singular where applicable.*

---

# PART ONE: KṚT PRATYAYAS
*Added directly to verbal roots (dhātu) to form nouns, adjectives, participles, and indeclinables.*

---

## 1. Gerundives — expressing necessity or fitness (future passive participles)

These three are among the most productive in Sanskrit and essential for expressing "should be done," "worth doing," "must be done."

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Formation | Examples |
|----------|---------------|-----------|---------|
| **-tavya** | tavyat | root + tavya | kartavya (to be done), gantavya (to be gone to), vaktavya (to be said), śrotavya (to be heard), bhoktavya (to be enjoyed), jñātavya (to be known) |
| **-anīya** | anīyar | root + anīya | karaṇīya (worthy of doing), śravaṇīya (worth hearing), darśanīya (worth seeing), vandanīya (worthy of worship), pūjanīya (worthy of worship) |
| **-ya** | ṇyat / yat | root + ya (with guṇa/vṛddhi) | kārya (to be done), vācya (to be said), pācya (to be cooked), pūjya (worthy of worship), mānyo (worthy of respect), śrāvya (worth hearing) |

*Note: -ya has multiple sources in Pāṇini and the exact conditioning is complex. For daily use, all three gerundives are interchangeable in meaning with subtle distinctions of register.*

---

## 2. Past Passive Participle (PPP)

The single most used kṛdanta form. Expresses completed action, often functions as a finite verb in classical prose.

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Formation | Examples |
|----------|---------------|-----------|---------|
| **-ta** | kta | root + ta (often with weakening) | kṛta (done), gata (gone), śruta (heard), ukta (said), datta (given), pakta (cooked), jñāta (known), labdha (obtained), mṛta (dead) |
| **-na** | kta (variant) | root + na (for certain roots) | lūna (cut), pūrṇa (full), bhinna (broken/split), chinna (cut), kṣīṇa (diminished) |
| **-ita** | kta (secondary/causative) | root + ita | calita (moved), bodhita (awakened), spandita (trembled), kalpita (imagined) |

*Note: The choice between -ta, -na, and -ita is root-specific. -ita is especially common with causative stems and loan/denominative roots.*

---

## 3. Present Participles

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Voice | Examples |
|----------|---------------|-------|---------|
| **-at / -ant** | śatṛ | active (parasmaipada) | pacat (cooking), gacchat (going), śṛṇvat (hearing), jānat (knowing), vadant (speaking) |
| **-māna** | śānac | middle (ātmanepada) | pacamāna, labhamāna (obtaining), sevamāna (serving), jāyamāna (being born) |
| **-āna** | śānac (perfect) | perfect middle | cakrāṇa (having done), ūcāna (having spoken) |

---

## 4. Perfect Active Participle

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Examples |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **-vas / -vat** | kvasu | vidvas (knowing — most common), cakṛvas (having done), jagmivas (having gone) |

*Note: vidvas (the wise one) is by far the most common in daily/literary use. Other forms are largely restricted to Vedic and epic.*

## 6. Absolutive / Gerund
*Indeclinable. Expresses a prior action: "having done X, then Y."*

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Usage | Examples |
|----------|---------------|-------|---------|
| **-tvā** | ktvā | without prefix | kṛtvā (having done), gatvā (having gone), śrutvā (having heard), dattvā (having given), uktvā (having said), jñātvā (having known) |
| **-ya** | lyap | with prefix | ādāya (having taken), nirgamya (having gone out), saṃśrutya (having heard together), abhidhāya (having said), vicārya (having considered) |

*This distinction — -tvā without prefix, -ya with prefix — is one of the clearest rules in Pāṇini and holds almost without exception.*

---

## 7. Infinitive

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Examples |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **-tum** | tumun | kartum (to do), gantum (to go), vaktum (to say), śrotum (to hear), draṣṭum (to see), jñātum (to know), dātum (to give), bhoktum (to enjoy) |

---

## 8. Agent Nouns (who performs the action)

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Meaning | Examples |
|----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| **-tṛ** | tṛc | primary agent (doer) | kartṛ (doer), dātṛ (giver), bhoktṛ (enjoyer), śrotṛ (listener), netṛ (leader), nartṛ (dancer), pitṛ (father/nourisher) |
| **-aka** | ṇvul | professional/habitual agent | pācaka (cook), nāyaka (leader/hero), gāyaka (singer), lekhakaḥ (writer), sevaka (servant), śiṣyaka... |
| **-in** | ṇini | one characterized by the action | yogin (one who practices yoga), tapasvin (ascetic), pakṣin (bird/winged one), dhanin (wealthy), balin (strong) |

*Note: -tṛ is the most grammatically basic agent noun. -aka tends toward professional identity. -in often implies habitual characteristic.*

---

## 9. Action Nouns (the act itself)

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Examples |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **-ana** | lyuṭ | karaṇa (doing/instrument), gamana (going), śravaṇa (hearing), darśana (seeing/philosophy), jñāna (knowing/knowledge), bhojana (eating/food), smaraṇa (remembering), vandana (salutation) |
| **-a** | ghañ / ac | pāka (cooking/digestion), yoga (union), tyāga (abandonment), bhoga (enjoyment), krodha (anger), śoka (grief), moha (delusion), bodha (awakening) |
| **-ti** | ktin | gati (going/motion), mati (thought/opinion), śakti (power), bhakti (devotion), yukti (reasoning/device), kīrti (fame), smṛti (memory), śruti (hearing/Veda), dṛṣṭi (vision) |
| **-man / -ma** | manin | karman (action), dharman → dharma, brahman (expansion/Brahma), nāman (name), janman (birth) |
| **-as** | asun | tapas (austerity/heat), manas (mind), śravas (fame), tejas (brilliance/energy), yaśas (glory), ojas (vitality) |
| **-tra** | ṣṭran | mantra (instrument of thought), tantra (loom/system), kṣetra (field), netra (eye/leader), pātra (vessel/worthy person) |

---

## 10. Miscellaneous Productive Kṛt Suffixes

| Pratyaya | Function | Examples |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **-uka** | tendency/disposition | bhāvuka (emotional), kāmuka (lustful), jalpuka (chattering) |
| **-u** | agent/quality (often Vedic) | śiśu (infant — one who lies down?), ripū... many are lexicalized |
| **-van** | agent (archaic/Vedic) | maghavan (generous one — Indra) |
| **-i** (kṛt) | agent noun | pati (master/lord), kavi (poet/seer) |

# PART TWO: TADDHITA PRATYAYAS
*Added to nouns, adjectives, or pronouns (not directly to verbal roots) to form secondary derivatives.*

---

## 1. Abstract Nouns — expressing quality or state

These two are the most productive taddhita suffixes in modern and classical Sanskrit.

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Examples |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **-tā** | tal | sundartā (beauty), satyatā (truthfulness), mahattā (greatness), mūrkhatā (foolishness), śūratā (heroism), ekatā (unity), kartṛtā (agenthood) |
| **-tva** | tval | sundaratva, satyatva, mahattva, kartṛtva, ātmatva (selfhood), brahmatva (Brahma-nature), manuṣyatva (humanity) |

*Both -tā and -tva form abstract nouns freely from almost any noun or adjective. -tva is slightly more philosophical in register; -tā more everyday. Both are fully productive in modern Sanskrit.*

---

## 2. "Made of" / "Consisting of"

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Examples |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **-maya** | mayaṭ | annamaya (made of food), ānandamaya (made of bliss), mṛṇmaya (made of clay), kāṣṭhamaya (made of wood), suvarnāmaya (made of gold), ānandasya maya (full of bliss) |

*Highly productive. Any material noun + -maya gives "consisting of / filled with."*

---

## 3. "Possessed of" / "Having"

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Examples |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **-vat / -mat** | matup / vatup | dhīmat (wise/having intelligence), śrīmat (having Śrī/blessed), bhagavat (having fortune/Lord), balavat (powerful), guṇavat (having qualities), buddhimat (having intelligence) |
| **-in** | ini | yogin, tapasvin (having austerity), dhanin (having wealth), balī (having strength), hastin (having a trunk/elephant), mālin (wearing a garland) |
| **-vant** | — | same as -vat above, just different stem forms |
| **-ila** | — | phenila (foamy), śīghraśīla... less productive |

*Note: -in and -vat/-mat overlap considerably. -in tends to imply "characterized by" while -vat/-mat implies "endowed with."*

---

## 4. Relation / Descent / Belonging

These form patronymics, derivatives, and relational adjectives.

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Examples |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **-a** | aṇ | pāṇḍava (of the Pāṇḍu line), mādhava (of Madhu's line), mānava (of Manu), āgneya (relating to Agni), saurya (relating to the sun) |
| **-eya** | ḍhak | kauṇṭeya (son of Kuntī), rādheya (son of Rādhā), gāṅgeya (born of Gaṅgā), āgneya (variant) |
| **-āyana** | phak/phañ | kātyāyana, vātsyāyana, śāṇḍilāyana |
| **-ya** | ṣyañ / yañ | āryā (noble), daivya (divine), mānuṣya (human), pārthya |
| **-ika** | ṭhak / ṭhañ | dhārmika (righteous), aupādhika, vyākaraṇika (relating to grammar), sāṃkhyika |

---

## 5. Comparative and Superlative

| Pratyaya | Technical name | Degree | Examples |
|----------|---------------|--------|---------|
| **-tara** | tarap | comparative | sundaratara (more beautiful), śīghratara (faster), uttaratara |
| **-tama** | tamap | superlative | sundaratama (most beautiful), uttama (highest), prathama (first) |
| **-īyas** | īyasun | comparative (stronger) | garīyas (heavier/more serious), śreyas (better), preyas (more dear), jyāyas (older/greater) |
| **-iṣṭha** | iṣṭhan | superlative (strongest) | gariṣṭha (heaviest), śreṣṭha (best/most excellent), jyeṣṭha (eldest), kaniṣṭha (youngest) |

*Note: -tara/-tama are the regular productive forms usable with any adjective. -īyas/-iṣṭha are the strong (suppletive) forms for specific roots and are largely lexicalized.*

---

## 6. Avyaya Taddhitas — forming indeclinables

These form adverbs from nouns and pronouns. Extremely important for daily usage.

| Pratyaya | Meaning | Examples |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **-tas** | from, in relation to | agratah (from the front), pārśvatah (from the side), śīrṣatah (from the head), mattah (from me), tasmāt (from that) |
| **-tra** | place where | tatra (there), atra (here), kutra (where?), yatra (where/wherever), sarvatra (everywhere), anyatra (elsewhere) |
| **-dā** | time when | tadā (then), kadā (when?), yadā (when/whenever), sadā (always), ekadā (once) |
| **-thā** | manner | tathā (thus/in that way), kathā → katham (how?), yathā (as/in whichever way) |
| **-dhā** | manner/multiplying | ekadhā (in one way), dvidhā (in two ways/divided), bahudhā (in many ways), viśvadhā |
| **-vat** | like, in the manner of | pitṛvat (like a father), guruvat (like a teacher), mātṛvat (like a mother) |

---

## 7. Numerical Derivatives

| Pratyaya | Meaning | Examples |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **-taya** | set/group of | dvitaya (pair/set of two), tritaya (set of three), catuṣṭaya (set of four) |
| **-guṇa** | -fold | dvigu (double), dviGuṇa (twofold), triGuṇa (threefold/the three guṇas), śataguṇa (hundredfold) |
| **-dhā** | divided into | ekadhā, dvidhā (see above) |
| **-śas** | distributive | ekaśas (one by one), śataśas (by hundreds), sahasraśas (by thousands) |

---

## 8. Diminutive / Affectionate

| Pratyaya | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **-ka** | putrika (little daughter), nagaraka (small town), bālaka (little child), rājaka (petty king), kuñjaraka (little elephant) |

*Note: -ka is also used as a purely expletive suffix (arthaless) in many words, which can make identifying true diminutives tricky.*

---

## Summary: The 10 Most Productive for Daily Use

If one had to choose the highest-yield pratyayas for active production:

1. **-ta** (kta) — past passive participle
2. **-tvā / -ya** (ktvā/lyap) — absolutive
3. **-tum** (tumun) — infinitive
4. **-ana** (lyuṭ) — action noun
5. **-tavya / -anīya / -ya** (gerundives) — obligation
6. **-tṛ** (tṛc) — agent noun
7. **-tā / -tva** (tal/tval) — abstract quality
8. **-maya** (mayaṭ) — made of / consisting of
9. **-vat / -mat** (matup) — possessed of
10. **-tara / -tama** (tarap/tamap) — comparative/superlative

# RESOURCES

## Primary Texts
- **Pāṇini's Aṣṭādhyāyī** — the source; kṛt pratyayas primarily in Adhyāya 3, taddhita in Adhyāya 4–5
- **Siddhāntakaumudī** (Bhaṭṭoji Dīkṣita, 17th c.) — systematic reorganization of Pāṇini by topic; the standard reference for learned study
- **Laghu Siddhāntakaumudī** (abridged Siddhāntakaumudī) — more accessible; recommended starting point

## Modern Grammars (English)
- **M.R. Kale, *A Higher Sanskrit Grammar*** — very thorough on both kṛt and taddhita with English explanations; the most practical English reference
- **William Dwight Whitney, *Sanskrit Grammar*** — rigorous and complete; excellent for looking up specific forms
- **A.A. Macdonell, *A Sanskrit Grammar for Students*** — more accessible than Whitney; good overview of taddhita
- **Thomas Egenes, *Introduction to Sanskrit*** (2 vols.) — pedagogically clear; covers main kṛdantas well

## Online
- **ashtadhyayi.com** — searchable Pāṇinian sūtras with commentary; essential for tracing any pratyaya to its rule
- **Sanskrit Grammar by Charles Wikner** (free PDF, widely available) — practical and clearly organized
- **learnsanskrit.cc** — good quick reference for forms

## For Sanskrit Scientific Vocabulary Specifically
- **Monier-Williams Sanskrit Dictionary** (monier-williams.me) — look up any derived form and trace back to root and suffix
- **Apte's Sanskrit Dictionary** — often more useful than Monier-Williams for classical usage examples
